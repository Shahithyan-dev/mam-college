Overview

M.A.M. School of Engineering, Siruganur, Tiruchirappalli is patronized by Maluk Educational Health and Charitable Trust. The trust has committed itself to the cause of education and pays special attention to impart technical education to the socially, educationally and economically backward communities.

The college was established on July 2010 with the approval of Tamilnadu Government and All India Council for Technical Education and is affiliated to Anna University, Chennai. In this competitive environment in the engineering education, MAMSE proves its commitment to provide quality education to their students.

VISION



To emerge as a Premier institute by empowering the students with competent knowledge, employable skills and research culture to satisfy the needs of the industry and society.



MISSION

To Inculcate conducive and innovative teaching learning environment

To equip the students with value and ethical based training to enhance the employable skills

To promote continuous learning and to facilitate exchange of innovative ideas through industry and institute collaborations

To imbibe communication skills, leadership skills, entrepreneurial skills and human values among the students.

About US

·       The office of The Controller of Examinations (COE) has been established from the month of June 2024 since the conferment of Autonomous to the institution.

·       The Office of the Controller of Examinations shall be responsible for assessing continuous learning process of the students by maintaining best quality and standard in examination process and ensuring confidentiality. It is the duty of the COE to arrange, prepare, schedule, conduct, publish and maintain records of IAT and End Semester Examinations of the students of all UG and PG programmes.

 

The other responsibilities of the office are:

·       Collection of student bio data from the students.

·       Mapping Electives from the students for the current semester.

·       Conducting Internal Assessment Examinations and End Semester Examinations for the students of various UG/PG Programmes.

·       Preparation of exam schedule, Hall allocation and Seating arrangements for IAT.

·       Collection of Question Papers from the departments, printing and disseminating for Internal Assessment Examinations and End Semester Examinations

·       Collecting Practical examination schedule from the departments.

·       Appointment of External Examiners for practical examinations.

·       Ensuring students attendance percentage during semester.

·       Lack of attendance, debarred and withdrawal Panel of Examiners preparation from various Institution/Universities.

·       Exam schedule preparation for Semester End Semester Examinations.

·       Appointment of Hall Invigilators, Hall Chart, Seating arrangements, Squad and Chief Superintendent etc.

·       Appointment of Evaluators, Chief Examiners and Chairman for Valuation.

·       Publication of Results.

·       Conducting Malpractice meeting, if any in the semester by Malpractice Committee.

·       Conducting of revaluation and issuing photocopy to the students.

·       Publishing revaluation results and Review process.

·       Conduction of Special Supplementary examination.

·       Printing of Statement of Grades, Consolidated Statement of Grades.

·       Issue of Duplicate Grade Sheet/ Certificate etc.,

·       Issue of Transcripts, CGPA to percentage conversion, Medium of Instruction, WES request etc.,

·       Conduction of graduation day and issuing degree certificate.

·       Assisting in student background verification.

E-Library

The E-Library at M.A.M.School of Engineering serves as a dynamic and comprehensive digital knowledge hub designed to meet the evolving academic and research needs of engineering students and faculty. Aligned with our vision of fostering excellence in education through technology, the E-Library provides seamless access to a wide array of electronic resources that support curriculum, innovation, and lifelong learning.

 

Key Features of our E-Library:

24/7 Access to Digital Resources

Students and faculty can access the E-Library round the clock via campus network or remote login, enabling flexible and uninterrupted learning.

Vast Collection of E-Resources

The E-Library hosts a rich repository of:

E-books across all engineering disciplines

National and international journals

E-magazines and technical newsletters

Project reports, theses, and dissertations

Multimedia content like video lectures, tutorials, and webinars

Subscribed Databases and Digital Platforms

The college subscribes to reputed databases and platforms such as:

J-Gate

DELNET / NDL (National Digital Library of India)

SWAYAM / NPTEL video lectures

·       E-ShodhSindhu

 

User-Friendly Interface

The E-Library platform is equipped with an intuitive and responsive interface, making it easy to search, browse, and download resources.

Support and Services:

Online public access catalog (OPAC)

Digital reference services

E-learning support and training sessions

Plagiarism checking tools

J-Gate

Overview:J-Gate is a comprehensive e-journal portal powered by Informatics India Ltd., serving as a single-window access point to millions of scholarly journal articles across various disciplines.

Key Features:

Indexes over 58 million journal articles from 50,000+ journals, with 11,000+ being open-access.

Covers subjects such as engineering, technology, science, humanities, social sciences, and management.

Features include advanced filtering, saved search alerts, citation export, and full-text linking to subscribed journals.

Academic Impact:

Enables faculty and students to perform in-depth literature reviews for projects and theses.

Promotes a research-oriented culture by providing access to the latest global publications.

Useful for plagiarism checks, referencing, and academic writing.

Website: https://jgatenext.com

DELNET – Developing Library Network

Overview:DELNET is a robust library network that facilitates resource sharing and inter-library loan services among over 7,000 member libraries across India and abroad.

Key Features:

Union catalogue containing:

Over 3 crore books.

Periodicals, thesis and dissertations, and CD-ROM databases.

Provides online requests for inter-library loans (ILL) and document delivery services (DDS).

Offers remote access to full-text journals, e-books, and institutional repositories.

Academic Impact:

Extends the reach of the institutional library beyond its physical boundaries.

Supports students and researchers by procuring rare or unavailable documents.

Encourages academic collaboration between institutions.

Website: https://delnet.in

INFLIBNET – Information and Library Network Centre

Overview:INFLIBNET is an Inter-University Centre of the University Grants Commission (UGC), focusing on promoting scholarly communication and digitization in higher education.

Key Services and Initiatives:

Shodhganga: Digital repository of Indian theses and dissertations.

Shodhgangotri: Repository of research proposals.

IRINS (Indian Research Information Network System): Showcases academic profiles and publication data of faculty members.

e-ShodhSindhu: Consortium for e-journals and e-books access to universities.

VIDWAN: Expert database and national research network.

Academic Impact:

Ensures visibility of faculty and student research outputs.

Enables students to refer to thousands of Indian PhD theses.

Assists in maintaining academic integrity and identifying research gaps.

Website: https://www.inflibnet.ac.in

NDLI – National Digital Library of India

Overview:NDLI is a Ministry of Education initiative hosted by IIT Kharagpur, aimed at creating a single-window access to educational resources from primary to postgraduate levels.

Key Features:

Over 7.5 crore digital resources including e-books, lecture videos, presentations, question papers, solutions, and simulations.

Content in multiple Indian languages and formats (text, audio, video, interactive).

Personal learning dashboard for individual learners.

Categorized learning resources for CBSE, NEET, JEE, UPSC, and higher education.

Academic Impact:

Promotes inclusive and multilingual learning.

Supports exam preparation, concept clarification, and skill-building.

Empowers students from rural and remote backgrounds with free access to national resources.

Website: https://ndl.iitkgp.ac.in

 

 

SWAYAM – Study Webs of Active-Learning for Young Aspiring Minds

Overview:SWAYAM is an online education platform developed by the Government of India to offer free learning courses from school to university level by the country’s top academic institutions.

Key Features:

Offers over 2,000 certified online courses across disciplines.

Developed by national coordinators like AICTE, UGC, CEC, NPTEL, IGNOU, IIMB.

Each course includes video lectures, reading materials, assignments, and discussion forums.

Students can earn certificates upon completion and passing proctored exams.

Academic Impact:

Courses are credit-transferable as per UGC regulations.

Supplements institutional curriculum and helps bridge skill gaps.

A platform for lifelong learning and faculty enrichment.

Website: https://swayam.gov.in

NPTEL – National Programme on Technology Enhanced Learning

Overview:NPTEL is a joint initiative by IITs and IISc, providing free online courses and certification in engineering, science, and humanities.

Key Features:

Over 600+ certification courses and 50,000+ video lectures in engineering, data science, artificial intelligence, soft skills, and more.

Courses are divided into 4, 8, or 12-week durations.

Offers industry-recognized certification exams, internships, and project opportunities.

Regularly updated to match AICTE/UGC curriculum.

Academic Impact:

Enhances classroom learning with expert-level instruction.

Boosts student confidence and skills for placements and GATE exams.

Faculty use it as a teaching aid or blended learning tool.

Website: https://nptel.ac.in

 

edX

Website: https://www.edx.org

Overview:edX is a global online learning platform founded by Harvard University and MIT in 2012. It offers high-quality courses from the world’s top universities and institutions. The platform provides a wide range of topics, including computer science, business, humanities, data science, and more. Learners can choose from free audit versions or pursue paid certificates and degrees.

Key Features:

Professional certificate programs, MicroMasters, and full online degrees.

Collaborations with top-tier institutions like Harvard, MIT, Berkeley, and Oxford.

Self-paced and instructor-led courses.

Interactive learning with quizzes, videos, and discussion forums.

 

Coursera

Website: https://www.coursera.org

Overview:Coursera is a leading online learning platform that partners with over 300 universities and companies, including Google, Stanford, and Yale, to offer courses, specializations, and degrees. Founded in 2012, Coursera is known for its user-friendly interface and a wide array of subjects, including data science, AI, personal development, and more.

Key Features:

Free access to many courses (with optional paid certificates).

Career-oriented professional certificates and full degrees.

Guided projects and peer-reviewed assignments.

Coursera Plus for unlimited access to thousands of courses.

 

Saylor Academy

Website: https://www.saylor.org

Overview:Saylor Academy is a nonprofit initiative working to make education freely accessible to all. It offers a range of college-level courses in subjects like business, computer science, economics, and mathematics. Learners can study at their own pace and even earn free certificates of completion.

Key Features:

Entirely free course catalog.

Open Educational Resources (OER)-based curriculum.

Proctored final exams for eligible certificates.

Transferable credits to select partner colleges.

 

Commonwealth of Learning (COL)

Website: https://www.col.org

Overview:The Commonwealth of Learning is an intergovernmental organization focused on promoting learning for sustainable development. It supports open and distance learning initiatives across Commonwealth countries. COL offers numerous free resources, courses, and toolkits for educators and learners.

Key Features:

Focus on lifelong learning and skills development.

Free courses via platforms like https://colcommons.org.

Emphasis on gender equality, education, and sustainable livelihoods.

Massive Open Online Courses (MOOCs) on development-oriented themes.

 

BCcampus OpenEd

Website: https://open.bccampus.ca

Overview:BCcampus OpenEd is a Canadian initiative that provides open educational resources (OER) to support post-secondary education. It focuses on reducing the cost of education by offering free, high-quality textbooks and resources for students and instructors.

Key Features:

Over 300 open textbooks.

Peer-reviewed and openly licensed content.

Designed primarily for post-secondary institutions in British Columbia.

Resources can be reused, revised, remixed, and redistributed.

 OpenStax

Website: https://openstax.org

Overview:OpenStax, a nonprofit educational initiative by Rice University, offers free, peer-reviewed, openly licensed textbooks for college and AP courses. Its mission is to increase student access to educational materials.

Key Features:

High-quality textbooks in subjects like physics, biology, economics, and sociology.

Free to download or view online; low-cost print versions available.

Customizable for instructors.

Aligned with standard curricula and learning outcomes.

 

LibreTexts

Website: https://libretexts.org

Overview:LibreTexts is a multi-institutional collaborative platform that develops open educational resources across a wide range of subjects. It serves as a central hub for textbooks, learning modules, and interactive simulations.

Key Features:

Covers subjects like chemistry, biology, engineering, social sciences, and humanities.

Supported by the U.S. Department of Education.

Dynamic and editable resources for educators.

Integration with LMS platforms and accessibility tools.

 

 SkillsCommons
